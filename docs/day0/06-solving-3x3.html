Warm-up: solving 3×3 systems by elimination

Goal

  • Solve three equations in three unknowns \(x\), \(y\), \(z\) using Gaussian elimination.
  • Practice writing the system as an augmented matrix and clearing it to upper-triangular form.
  • Finish every problem with back-substitution and a check. The written exam rewards showing each row operation.

The method

A 3×3 system \(A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b}\) is

\[ \begin{aligned} a_{11}x + a_{12}y + a_{13}z &= b_1 \\ a_{21}x + a_{22}y + a_{23}z &= b_2 \\ a_{31}x + a_{32}y + a_{33}z &= b_3 \end{aligned} \]

We pack the coefficients and the right-hand side into an augmented matrix:

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} a_{11} & a_{12} & a_{13} & b_1 \\ a_{21} & a_{22} & a_{23} & b_2 \\ a_{31} & a_{32} & a_{33} & b_3 \end{array}\right]. \]

Gaussian elimination uses row operations to create zeros below the diagonal:

  1. Use row 1 to make the entries below \(a_{11}\) zero (operations like \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - kR_1\)).
  2. Use row 2 to make the entry below \(a_{22}\) zero (\(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - kR_2\)).
  3. You now have an upper-triangular matrix. The bottom row reads off \(z\) directly.
  4. Back-substitute: get \(z\), then \(y\), then \(x\).
  5. Check by plugging \(x,y,z\) into all three original equations.

A legal row operation is: replace a row by (that row) \(\pm\) (a multiple of another row). It does not change the solution.

Tiny worked example

\[ \begin{aligned} x + y + z &= 6 \\ \phantom{x} + y + z &= 5 \\ \phantom{x + y} + z &= 3 \end{aligned} \qquad \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 3 \end{array}\right] \]

This is already upper-triangular. Back-substitute from the bottom:

  • Row 3: \(z = 3\).
  • Row 2: \(y + z = 5 \Rightarrow y + 3 = 5 \Rightarrow y = 2\).
  • Row 1: \(x + y + z = 6 \Rightarrow x + 2 + 3 = 6 \Rightarrow x = 1\).

So \(x = 1,\ y = 2,\ z = 3\). Check: \(1+2+3=6\) ✓, \(2+3=5\) ✓, \(z=3\) ✓.

Drills

Write each system as an augmented matrix, eliminate to upper-triangular form showing each row operation, then back-substitute and check. Drills 1–3 are already triangular (back-substitution only). Drills 4–6 need one elimination step. The rest need two or more.

1. \[ \begin{aligned} x + y + z &= 9 \\ y + z &= 7 \\ z &= 4 \end{aligned} \]

2. \[ \begin{aligned} x + 2y + z &= 10 \\ y - z &= 1 \\ z &= 2 \end{aligned} \]

3. \[ \begin{aligned} 2x + y + z &= 11 \\ 3y + z &= 8 \\ 2z &= 6 \end{aligned} \]

4. \[ \begin{aligned} x + y + z &= 6 \\ 2x + 3y + z &= 11 \\ z &= 3 \end{aligned} \]

5. \[ \begin{aligned} x + y + z &= 6 \\ x + 2y + z &= 8 \\ 2y + 3z &= 13 \end{aligned} \]

6. \[ \begin{aligned} x - y + z &= 2 \\ 2x + y + z &= 7 \\ y + 2z &= 5 \end{aligned} \]

7. \[ \begin{aligned} x + y + z &= 6 \\ x + 2y + 3z &= 14 \\ x + 4y + 9z &= 36 \end{aligned} \]

8. \[ \begin{aligned} x + y + z &= 6 \\ 2x - y + z &= 3 \\ x + 2y - z &= 3 \end{aligned} \]

9. \[ \begin{aligned} x + y + z &= 4 \\ 2x - y + z &= 7 \\ x + 2y + z &= 3 \end{aligned} \]

10. \[ \begin{aligned} x + 2y - z &= 2 \\ 2x - y + z &= 3 \\ -x + y + 2z &= 6 \end{aligned} \]

Check in R

Verify any answer with solve. Build the coefficient matrix A row-by-row with byrow = TRUE, the right-hand side b, then call solve(A, b).

Drill 7:

A <- matrix(c(1, 1, 1,
              1, 2, 3,
              1, 4, 9), nrow = 3, byrow = TRUE)
b <- c(6, 14, 36)
solve(A, b)
#> [1] 1 2 3      # x = 1, y = 2, z = 3

Drill 9:

A <- matrix(c(1, 1, 1,
              2, -1, 1,
              1, 2, 1), nrow = 3, byrow = TRUE)
b <- c(4, 7, 3)
solve(A, b)
#> [1] 1 -1 4     # x = 1, y = -1, z = 4

Hints

Hint 1 — drills 1–3 (already triangular)

No row operations needed. Read \(z\) from the bottom row, substitute up into the middle row to get \(y\), then into the top row to get \(x\). In drill 3 the bottom row is \(2z = 6\), so divide by 2 first.

Hint 2 — drills 4–6 (one elimination)

Only one entry below the diagonal is nonzero. In drill 4, use \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1\) to kill the \(2x\). In drill 5, use \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - R_1\). After that single step the matrix is triangular and you can back-substitute.

Hint 3 — drills 7–8

Two eliminations under column 1, then one under column 2. Keep the pivot (top-left \(1\)) and subtract the right multiple of \(R_1\) from \(R_2\) and \(R_3\) so both \(x\)-entries become 0. Then use the new \(R_2\) to clear the \(y\)-entry in \(R_3\).

Hint 4 — drills 9–10

The top-left pivot is already a \(1\). Clear column 1 with \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1\) and \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 \pm R_1\), then clear column 2 with a multiple of the new \(R_2\). The middle pivot may be negative (like \(-3\) in drill 9); that is fine — just keep track of signs. Drill 10 lands on simple fractions over \(14\), which is expected.

Step-by-step solutions

Solutions

Drill 1

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 7 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 4 \end{array}\right] \] Already triangular. Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(z = 4\).
  • Row 2: \(y + z = 7 \Rightarrow y + 4 = 7 \Rightarrow y = 3\).
  • Row 1: \(x + y + z = 9 \Rightarrow x + 3 + 4 = 9 \Rightarrow x = 2\).

Answer: \(x = 2,\ y = 3,\ z = 4\). Check: \(2+3+4=9\) ✓, \(3+4=7\) ✓, \(z=4\) ✓.

Drill 2

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 1 & 10 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(z = 2\).
  • Row 2: \(y - z = 1 \Rightarrow y - 2 = 1 \Rightarrow y = 3\).
  • Row 1: \(x + 2y + z = 10 \Rightarrow x + 6 + 2 = 10 \Rightarrow x = 2\).

Answer: \(x = 2,\ y = 3,\ z = 2\). Check: \(2 + 2(3) + 2 = 10\) ✓, \(3 - 2 = 1\) ✓, \(z=2\) ✓.

Drill 3

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 2 & 1 & 1 & 11 \\ 0 & 3 & 1 & 8 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 6 \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(2z = 6 \Rightarrow z = 3\).
  • Row 2: \(3y + z = 8 \Rightarrow 3y + 3 = 8 \Rightarrow 3y = 5 \Rightarrow y = \tfrac{5}{3}\).
  • Row 1: \(2x + y + z = 11 \Rightarrow 2x + \tfrac{5}{3} + 3 = 11 \Rightarrow 2x = 11 - \tfrac{14}{3} = \tfrac{19}{3} \Rightarrow x = \tfrac{19}{6}\).

Answer: \(x = \tfrac{19}{6},\ y = \tfrac{5}{3},\ z = 3\). Check: \(2(\tfrac{19}{6}) + \tfrac{5}{3} + 3 = \tfrac{19}{3} + \tfrac{5}{3} + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11\) ✓, \(3(\tfrac{5}{3}) + 3 = 5 + 3 = 8\) ✓, \(2(3) = 6\) ✓.

Drill 4

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 2 & 3 & 1 & 11 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 3 \end{array}\right] \] Eliminate the \(2\) in row 2, column 1: \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1\). \[ R_2:\ (2-2,\ 3-2,\ 1-2 \mid 11-12) = (0,\ 1,\ -1 \mid -1). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 3 \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(z = 3\).
  • Row 2: \(y - z = -1 \Rightarrow y - 3 = -1 \Rightarrow y = 2\).
  • Row 1: \(x + y + z = 6 \Rightarrow x + 2 + 3 = 6 \Rightarrow x = 1\).

Answer: \(x = 1,\ y = 2,\ z = 3\). Check: \(1+2+3=6\) ✓, \(2(1)+3(2)+3 = 2+6+3 = 11\) ✓, \(z=3\) ✓.

Drill 5

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 & 8 \\ 0 & 2 & 3 & 13 \end{array}\right] \] Eliminate the \(1\) in row 2, column 1: \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - R_1\). \[ R_2:\ (1-1,\ 2-1,\ 1-1 \mid 8-6) = (0,\ 1,\ 0 \mid 2). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 2 & 3 & 13 \end{array}\right] \] Now clear the \(2\) in row 3, column 2: \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 2R_2\). \[ R_3:\ (0,\ 2-2,\ 3-0 \mid 13-4) = (0,\ 0,\ 3 \mid 9). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 3 & 9 \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(3z = 9 \Rightarrow z = 3\).
  • Row 2: \(y = 2\).
  • Row 1: \(x + y + z = 6 \Rightarrow x + 2 + 3 = 6 \Rightarrow x = 1\).

Answer: \(x = 1,\ y = 2,\ z = 3\). Check: \(1+2+3=6\) ✓, \(1+2(2)+3 = 1+4+3 = 8\) ✓, \(2(2)+3(3) = 4+9 = 13\) ✓.

Drill 6

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & 1 & 2 \\ 2 & 1 & 1 & 7 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \end{array}\right] \] Eliminate the \(2\) in row 2, column 1: \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1\). \[ R_2:\ (2-2,\ 1-(-2),\ 1-2 \mid 7-4) = (0,\ 3,\ -1 \mid 3). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 3 & -1 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 5 \end{array}\right] \] Clear the \(1\) in row 3, column 2: \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - \tfrac{1}{3}R_2\). \[ R_3:\ \left(0,\ 1-1,\ 2-(-\tfrac{1}{3}) \,\middle|\, 5-1\right) = \left(0,\ 0,\ \tfrac{7}{3} \,\middle|\, 4\right). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & -1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 3 & -1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & \tfrac{7}{3} & 4 \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(\tfrac{7}{3}z = 4 \Rightarrow z = \tfrac{12}{7}\).
  • Row 2: \(3y - z = 3 \Rightarrow 3y = 3 + \tfrac{12}{7} = \tfrac{33}{7} \Rightarrow y = \tfrac{11}{7}\).
  • Row 1: \(x - y + z = 2 \Rightarrow x = 2 + y - z = 2 + \tfrac{11}{7} - \tfrac{12}{7} = 2 - \tfrac{1}{7} = \tfrac{13}{7}\).

Answer: \(x = \tfrac{13}{7},\ y = \tfrac{11}{7},\ z = \tfrac{12}{7}\). Check: \(\tfrac{13}{7} - \tfrac{11}{7} + \tfrac{12}{7} = \tfrac{14}{7} = 2\) ✓; \(2(\tfrac{13}{7}) + \tfrac{11}{7} + \tfrac{12}{7} = \tfrac{26+11+12}{7} = \tfrac{49}{7} = 7\) ✓; \(\tfrac{11}{7} + 2(\tfrac{12}{7}) = \tfrac{11+24}{7} = \tfrac{35}{7} = 5\) ✓.

Drill 7

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 & 14 \\ 1 & 4 & 9 & 36 \end{array}\right] \] Clear column 1: \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - R_1\) and \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - R_1\). \[ R_2:\ (0,\ 1,\ 2 \mid 8), \qquad R_3:\ (0,\ 3,\ 8 \mid 30). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 8 \\ 0 & 3 & 8 & 30 \end{array}\right] \] Clear column 2 below the pivot: \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - 3R_2\). \[ R_3:\ (0,\ 3-3,\ 8-6 \mid 30-24) = (0,\ 0,\ 2 \mid 6). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 & 8 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 6 \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(2z = 6 \Rightarrow z = 3\).
  • Row 2: \(y + 2z = 8 \Rightarrow y + 6 = 8 \Rightarrow y = 2\).
  • Row 1: \(x + y + z = 6 \Rightarrow x + 2 + 3 = 6 \Rightarrow x = 1\).

Answer: \(x = 1,\ y = 2,\ z = 3\). Check: \(1+2+3=6\) ✓; \(1 + 2(2) + 3(3) = 1+4+9 = 14\) ✓; \(1 + 4(2) + 9(3) = 1+8+27 = 36\) ✓.

Drill 8

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 2 & -1 & 1 & 3 \\ 1 & 2 & -1 & 3 \end{array}\right] \] Clear column 1: \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1\) and \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - R_1\). \[ R_2:\ (0,\ -3,\ -1 \mid -9), \qquad R_3:\ (0,\ 1,\ -2 \mid -3). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 0 & -3 & -1 & -9 \\ 0 & 1 & -2 & -3 \end{array}\right] \] Clear column 2 below the pivot: \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + \tfrac{1}{3}R_2\). \[ R_3:\ \left(0,\ 1 + \tfrac{1}{3}(-3),\ -2 + \tfrac{1}{3}(-1) \,\middle|\, -3 + \tfrac{1}{3}(-9)\right) = \left(0,\ 0,\ -\tfrac{7}{3} \,\middle|\, -6\right). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 6 \\ 0 & -3 & -1 & -9 \\ 0 & 0 & -\tfrac{7}{3} & -6 \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(-\tfrac{7}{3}z = -6 \Rightarrow z = \tfrac{18}{7}\).
  • Row 2: \(-3y - z = -9 \Rightarrow -3y = -9 + \tfrac{18}{7} = -\tfrac{45}{7} \Rightarrow y = \tfrac{15}{7}\).
  • Row 1: \(x + y + z = 6 \Rightarrow x = 6 - \tfrac{15}{7} - \tfrac{18}{7} = 6 - \tfrac{33}{7} = \tfrac{9}{7}\).

Answer: \(x = \tfrac{9}{7},\ y = \tfrac{15}{7},\ z = \tfrac{18}{7}\). Check: \(\tfrac{9+15+18}{7} = \tfrac{42}{7} = 6\) ✓; \(2(\tfrac{9}{7}) - \tfrac{15}{7} + \tfrac{18}{7} = \tfrac{18-15+18}{7} = \tfrac{21}{7} = 3\) ✓; \(\tfrac{9}{7} + 2(\tfrac{15}{7}) - \tfrac{18}{7} = \tfrac{9+30-18}{7} = \tfrac{21}{7} = 3\) ✓.

Drill 9

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 4 \\ 2 & -1 & 1 & 7 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 & 3 \end{array}\right] \] Clear column 1: \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1\) and \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 - R_1\). \[ R_2:\ (2-2,\ -1-2,\ 1-2 \mid 7-8) = (0,\ -3,\ -1 \mid -1), \] \[ R_3:\ (1-1,\ 2-1,\ 1-1 \mid 3-4) = (0,\ 1,\ 0 \mid -1). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & -3 & -1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -1 \end{array}\right] \] Clear column 2 below the pivot: \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + \tfrac{1}{3}R_2\). \[ R_3:\ \left(0,\ 1 + \tfrac{1}{3}(-3),\ 0 + \tfrac{1}{3}(-1) \,\middle|\, -1 + \tfrac{1}{3}(-1)\right) = \left(0,\ 0,\ -\tfrac{1}{3} \,\middle|\, -\tfrac{4}{3}\right). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 1 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & -3 & -1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & -\tfrac{1}{3} & -\tfrac{4}{3} \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(-\tfrac{1}{3}z = -\tfrac{4}{3} \Rightarrow z = 4\).
  • Row 2: \(-3y - z = -1 \Rightarrow -3y - 4 = -1 \Rightarrow -3y = 3 \Rightarrow y = -1\).
  • Row 1: \(x + y + z = 4 \Rightarrow x - 1 + 4 = 4 \Rightarrow x = 1\).

Answer: \(x = 1,\ y = -1,\ z = 4\). Check: \(1 + (-1) + 4 = 4\) ✓; \(2(1) - (-1) + 4 = 2 + 1 + 4 = 7\) ✓; \(1 + 2(-1) + 4 = 1 - 2 + 4 = 3\) ✓.

Drill 10

\[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & -1 & 2 \\ 2 & -1 & 1 & 3 \\ -1 & 1 & 2 & 6 \end{array}\right] \] Clear column 1: \(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - 2R_1\) and \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + R_1\). \[ R_2:\ (0,\ -5,\ 3 \mid -1), \qquad R_3:\ (0,\ 3,\ 1 \mid 8). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & -1 & 2 \\ 0 & -5 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 3 & 1 & 8 \end{array}\right] \] Clear column 2 below the pivot: \(R_3 \leftarrow R_3 + \tfrac{3}{5}R_2\). \[ R_3:\ \left(0,\ 3 + \tfrac{3}{5}(-5),\ 1 + \tfrac{3}{5}(3) \,\middle|\, 8 + \tfrac{3}{5}(-1)\right) = \left(0,\ 0,\ \tfrac{14}{5} \,\middle|\, \tfrac{37}{5}\right). \] \[ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & -1 & 2 \\ 0 & -5 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & \tfrac{14}{5} & \tfrac{37}{5} \end{array}\right] \] Back-substitute:

  • Row 3: \(\tfrac{14}{5}z = \tfrac{37}{5} \Rightarrow z = \tfrac{37}{14}\).
  • Row 2: \(-5y + 3z = -1 \Rightarrow -5y = -1 - 3(\tfrac{37}{14}) = -1 - \tfrac{111}{14} = -\tfrac{125}{14} \Rightarrow y = \tfrac{125}{70} = \tfrac{25}{14}\).
  • Row 1: \(x + 2y - z = 2 \Rightarrow x = 2 - 2(\tfrac{25}{14}) + \tfrac{37}{14} = 2 - \tfrac{50}{14} + \tfrac{37}{14} = 2 - \tfrac{13}{14} = \tfrac{15}{14}\).

Answer: \(x = \tfrac{15}{14},\ y = \tfrac{25}{14},\ z = \tfrac{37}{14}\). Check: (row 1) \(\tfrac{15}{14} + 2(\tfrac{25}{14}) - \tfrac{37}{14} = \tfrac{15 + 50 - 37}{14} = \tfrac{28}{14} = 2\) ✓; (row 2) \(2(\tfrac{15}{14}) - \tfrac{25}{14} + \tfrac{37}{14} = \tfrac{30 - 25 + 37}{14} = \tfrac{42}{14} = 3\) ✓; (row 3) \(-\tfrac{15}{14} + \tfrac{25}{14} + 2(\tfrac{37}{14}) = \tfrac{-15 + 25 + 74}{14} = \tfrac{84}{14} = 6\) ✓.
