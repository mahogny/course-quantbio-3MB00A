Quiz: vectors, dot product & projection

Short questions to test your geometric intuition. Try each on paper first, then open the answer. Several are written in the style of the on-campus exam (no computer).

Q1. A gene-expression profile of 12 000 genes is a vector. In how many dimensions does it “live”?

Hint

The dimension equals the number of components (measurements).

Answer

12 000 dimensions — one axis per gene. We cannot draw it, but every rule from 2-D and 3-D still applies.

Q2. Compute \(\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix} + 3\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}\).

Hint

Scale first, then add componentwise.

Answer

\(3(1,2) = (3,6)\), then \((2,-1)+(3,6) = (5,\ 5)\).

Q3. What is the length of \(\begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix}\)?

Hint

\(\|\mathbf{v}\| = \sqrt{v_1^2 + v_2^2}\).

Answer

\(\sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = 10\).

Q4. Compute the dot product \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}\cdot\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 0 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}\).

Hint

Multiply matching components and add.

Answer

\(1\cdot4 + 3\cdot0 + (-2)\cdot5 = 4 + 0 - 10 = -6\).

Q5. The dot product of two non-zero vectors is negative. What does that tell you about the angle between them?

Hint

Recall \(\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v} = \|\mathbf{u}\|\|\mathbf{v}\|\cos\theta\), and lengths are positive.

Answer

Since the lengths are positive, a negative dot product forces \(\cos\theta < 0\), so the angle is greater than \(90^\circ\) — the vectors point in broadly opposite directions.

Q6. Are \(\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\) orthogonal?

Hint

Orthogonal iff the dot product is exactly 0.

Answer

\(3(-1) + 1\cdot3 = -3 + 3 = 0\) → yes, orthogonal (a right angle).

Q7. Two samples have profiles \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v} = 5\mathbf{u}\). What is their cosine similarity, and why is that biologically reassuring?

Hint

\(\mathbf{v}\) is just \(\mathbf{u}\) scaled. Does scaling change direction?

Answer

Cosine similarity = 1. Scaling does not change direction, so two profiles that differ only by overall magnitude (e.g. sequencing depth) are correctly judged identical in shape. Cosine similarity is depth-invariant.

Q8. True or false: the dot product of two vectors is itself a vector.

Hint

Count the numbers in the result.

Answer

False. The dot product is a single scalar (one number).

Q9. Project \(\mathbf{u} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}\) onto \(\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\).

Hint

\(\text{proj}_{\mathbf{v}}\mathbf{u} = \dfrac{\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}}{\mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{v}}\,\mathbf{v}\). Here \(\mathbf{v}\) is the x-axis.

Answer

\(\dfrac{\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}}{\mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{v}} = \dfrac{5}{1} = 5\), so the projection is \(5(1,0) = (5,\ 0)\) — the shadow on the x-axis. (Makes sense: projecting onto the x-axis just keeps the x-component.)

Q10. After projecting \(\mathbf{u}\) onto \(\mathbf{v}\), what is special about the leftover \(\mathbf{u} - \text{proj}_{\mathbf{v}}\mathbf{u}\)?

Hint

Think about the right angle a shadow makes with the ground.

Answer

It is orthogonal to \(\mathbf{v}\) (dot product 0). The projection captures the part of \(\mathbf{u}\) along \(\mathbf{v}\); the leftover is the part with no overlap with \(\mathbf{v}\). This is the seed of PCA and least-squares regression.

Q11. Which R expression computes the dot product of vectors u and v? (a) u * v (b) sum(u * v) (c) u %o% v

Hint

A dot product returns a single number. * is elementwise; %o% is the outer product.

Answer
  1. sum(u * v). Option (a) gives the elementwise products (a vector), and %o% gives an outer-product matrix. u %*% v also works, returning a \(1\times1\) matrix.

Q12. A sample \(\mathbf{u}\) has cosine similarity 0 with sample \(\mathbf{v}\). Interpret this.

Hint

\(\cos\theta = 0\) means what angle?

Answer

The profiles are orthogonal (\(\theta = 90^\circ\)): as different in direction as possible without pointing opposite. The two samples share no common expression pattern in this geometric sense.
