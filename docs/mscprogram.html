Master’s Programme in Bioinformatics (Umeå University)

Source: https://www.umu.se/en/education/programmes/masters-programme-in-bioinformatics/

This course — Quantitative biology and integrative omics (3MB00A) — is one course within this two-year (120 ECTS) Master’s programme. This file records the surrounding curriculum so we can calibrate what students already know when they reach us, and what they will go on to do.

Programme overview

“How data science, computational and statistical approaches are applied to enable novel biological insights.” A broad insight into the use of bioinformatics, from clinical genetics to plant biotechnology and biodiversity monitoring.

  • Degree: Master’s, 120 ECTS (2 years)
  • Year 1 focus: foundational biostatistics, practical computing and scripting, and the generation and analysis of high-throughput genomics data.

Year 1 courses (in programme order)

Per the course author, the three courses that run before 3MB00A are the first three below. Statistics and machine learning in bioinformatics runs concurrently or after — it is not a prerequisite.

# Course Relation to 3MB00A
1 Computing for data driven biology before
2 Statistics and data science for biologists before
3 Programming in Python for bioinformatics before
4 Statistics and machine learning in bioinformatics concurrent / after — not a prerequisite
5 Quantitative biology and integrative omics (3MB00A) ← this course
6 Comparative genomics after
7 Functional genomics theory after
8 Applied functional genomics after

Year 2 courses

  • Applied bioinformatics
  • Clinical omics and bioinformatics
  • Epigenetics and the non-coding genome
  • Microbial bioinformatics
  • Exam thesis in bioinformatics

What this means for our course

By the time programme students reach 3MB00A, they have already taken three courses that are directly relevant:

  • Computing for data driven biology — practical computing / scripting.
  • Statistics and data science for biologists — distributions, estimation, data wrangling, plotting.
  • Programming in Python for bioinformatics — they can program (Python, and R from the entry requirement).

So our typical student is not a programming or statistics novice. But note that Statistics and machine learning in bioinformatics is not a prerequisite — so we should not assume prior exposure to machine learning or to PCA / dimensional reduction; those are things 3MB00A itself introduces. See audience.md for how this shapes what we assume.

However, the course syllabus is written so the course can also be taken stand-alone: the formal entry requirement is only 90 ECTS in biology (or related) plus 5 ECTS of programming in R or Python. Material must therefore not hard-depend on the four preceding courses, even though most students will have taken them.
