# Course syllabus — 3MB00A

Source: <https://www.umu.se/en/education/syllabus/3mb00a/>

## Course identification

- **English title:** Quantitative Biology and Integrative Biology
- **Swedish title:** Kvantitativ biologi och integrativ omik
- **Course code:** 3MB00A
- **Credits:** 7.5 ECTS
- **Education level:** Second cycle
- **Main field of study:** Bioinformatics (second cycle, entry-level requirements only)
- **Grading scale:** Three-grade scale (U / G / VG)
- **Responsible department:** Department of Molecular Biology
- **Validity:** 2026-03-09 and until further notice

## Course content

The course focuses on basic mathematical and statistical methods that are widely
used within modern bioinformatics. Core topics:

- Linear algebra — matrices, vectors, eigenvalues
- Markov chains
- Dimensional reduction techniques — PCA and OLS
- Bayesian statistics
- Statistical models common within bioinformatics (e.g. the Poisson and negative
  binomial distributions)
- Applications in chemometrics and single-cell analysis

## Learning outcomes

**Knowledge and understanding.** Students should be able to:

- explain statistical distributions
- distinguish Bayesian from frequentist approaches
- describe solution spaces for linear systems
- understand latent space concepts

**Skills and abilities.** Students should be able to:

- solve systems of linear equations
- formulate biological problems using Markov chains
- analyse large-scale data using latent space methods
- apply chemometrics to biological datasets

**Judgement and approach.** Students should be able to:

- assess the validity of mathematical models for biological systems

## Entry requirements

90 ECTS from completed courses in the fields of biology, biomedicine, molecular
biology, molecular ecology, molecular evolution, genetics, or other related
subjects; 5 credits in programming in R or Python.

## Instruction and examination

Campus-based, delivered in English (Swedish if applicable), comprising lectures
and teacher-led guided sessions. Examination is by an individual written on-campus
exam. Pass (G) requires passing the written exam; distinction (VG) requires
distinction-level performance. Optional bonus points (up to 10 % of total score)
may be awarded for participation.
