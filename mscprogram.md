# Master's Programme in Bioinformatics (Umeå University)

Source: <https://www.umu.se/en/education/programmes/masters-programme-in-bioinformatics/>

This course — **Quantitative biology and integrative omics (3MB00A)** — is one
course within this two-year (120 ECTS) Master's programme. This file records the
surrounding curriculum so we can calibrate what students already know when they
reach us, and what they will go on to do.

## Programme overview

> "How data science, computational and statistical approaches are applied to
> enable novel biological insights." A broad insight into the use of
> bioinformatics, from clinical genetics to plant biotechnology and biodiversity
> monitoring.

- **Degree:** Master's, 120 ECTS (2 years)
- **Year 1 focus:** foundational biostatistics, practical computing and scripting,
  and the generation and analysis of high-throughput genomics data.

## What this means for our course

By the time programme students reach **3MB00A**, they have already taken three
courses that are directly relevant:

- **Computing for data driven biology** — practical computing / scripting.
- **Statistics and data science for biologists** — distributions, estimation,
  data wrangling, plotting.
- **Programming in Python for bioinformatics** — they can program (Python, and R
  from the entry requirement).

So our typical student is **not** a programming or statistics novice. But note that
*Statistics and machine learning in bioinformatics* is **not** a prerequisite — so
we should **not** assume prior exposure to machine learning or to PCA / dimensional
reduction; those are things 3MB00A itself introduces. See [audience.md](audience.md)
for how this shapes what we assume.

**However**, the [course syllabus](courseplan.md) is written so the course can also
be taken stand-alone: the formal entry requirement is only 90 ECTS in biology (or
related) plus 5 ECTS of programming in R or Python. Material must therefore not
*hard-depend* on the four preceding courses, even though most students will have
taken them.
